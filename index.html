Stage 1 – Beginner: What is an Expression?

Variable: A letter (like x or y) that stands for an unknown number.

Coefficient: The number multiplying a variable (e.g., the 3 in 3x).

Term: A single mathematical building block (e.g., 5x, 2, or y).

Expression: Code blocks built out of terms joined together (e.g., 3x + 2).

Understanding Brackets

In algebra, brackets mean "multiply everything inside!"

Example: 3(x + 2)
Step 1: Multiply 3 by x → 3x
Step 2: Multiply 3 by 2 → 6
Expanded Result = 3x + 6

Stage 2 – Explorer: Guided Practice

Watch how the outside multiplier distributes evenly to both individual items inside:

Example: 4(a + 5)
• 4 times a → 4a
• 4 times 5 → 20
Resulting Expression = 4a + 20

Multi-Step: The Extra Term

What if there is an extra term hanging around outside the brackets?

Expand & Simplify: 2(x + 3) + x
Step 1 (Expand bracket): 2x + 6 + x
Step 2 (Group like x terms): 2x + x + 6
Final Answer = 3x + 6

Watching Your Operation Signs

Be careful tracking signs during combining cleanups:

Expand & Simplify: 5(y + 1) − 2y
Step 1 (Multiply Bracket): 5y + 5 − 2y
Step 2 (Combine the y terms): 5y − 2y + 5
Final Answer = 3y + 5

Stage 3 – Builder: Practice Time!

Question: Expand and simplify: 4(n + 3) − 2n

Stage 4 – Advanced: Perimeter Problem

A rectangular field has a length of (x + 3) meters and a width of (x + 1) meters. What is the total combined perimeter expression?

Hint: Perimeter = 2(Length) + 2(Width)

Total Perimeter = 2(x + 3) + 2(x + 1)
Expand terms → 2x + 6 + 2x + 2
Group & Combine → 4x + 8 meters!
